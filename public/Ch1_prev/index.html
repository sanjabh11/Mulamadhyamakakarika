Chapter 1/14

Rejection of Inherent Existence (svabhava)

Madhyamaka Concept

The rejection of inherent existence (svabhava) is a fundamental principle in Madhyamaka philosophy, asserting that phenomena lack fixed, independent essence.

Quantum Parallel

The Copenhagen interpretation suggests particles have no definite properties until measured, existing in probabilistic states rather than fixed realities.

Parallel Rationale

Both challenge fixed, inherent properties: Madhyamaka denies intrinsic existence, while the Copenhagen view shows particles lack definite states until observed.
